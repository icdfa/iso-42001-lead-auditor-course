## Assignment Overview

The rapid proliferation of Artificial Intelligence across critical sectors -- including healthcare, financial services, criminal justice, and public administration -- has intensified global debate about AI governance, accountability, and ethics. ISO/IEC 42001:2023 represents the international community's most comprehensive attempt to standardize the governance of AI through a management system framework.

This assignment asks you to investigate a **real-world AI governance failure or controversy** of your choice and critically analyze it through the lens of ISO/IEC 42001. You will then evaluate the standard's effectiveness as a governance tool and propose a forward-looking AIMS implementation strategy for the organization involved.

## Part A: Selecting Your Case (No Marks -- Prerequisite)

Before beginning your research, select **one** real-world AI governance failure or controversy from the list below, or propose your own (subject to approval). Your chosen case must involve a specific organization and a specific AI system.

**Suggested Cases:**
1. **Amazon's AI Recruiting Tool (2018):** The AI system that was found to discriminate against female job applicants.
2. **COMPAS Recidivism Algorithm (USA):** The AI tool used in criminal sentencing that was found to be racially biased against Black defendants.

6. **A case of your own choosing** (must be pre-approved by your instructor).

## Part B: Report Structure and Tasks

Your report must be structured as follows:

---

### Section 1: Introduction (10 Marks)

Write a concise introduction that:
1. Identifies the organization and the specific AI system at the center of your case study.
2. Provides a brief contextual background of the organization and the intended purpose of the AI system.
3. Clearly states the AI governance failure or controversy and its significance.
4. Outlines the structure of the rest of your report.

*Marking Criteria: Clarity and conciseness of the introduction; accuracy of the background information; quality of sourcing.*

---

### Section 2: Critical Analysis of the AI Governance Failure (30 Marks)

This is the core analytical section of your report. You must conduct a thorough, evidence-based analysis of what went wrong. Your analysis must address the following:

**2.1 Root Cause Analysis**
Identify and critically analyze the underlying root causes of the AI governance failure. Go beyond the surface-level symptoms. Consider technical causes (e.g., biased training data, flawed algorithm design), organizational causes (e.g., lack of accountability, inadequate testing), and cultural causes (e.g., commercial pressure overriding ethical considerations).

**2.2 Stakeholder Impact Assessment**
Identify the key stakeholders who were affected by the AI governance failure. For each stakeholder group, describe the specific harm or negative impact they experienced. Consider both direct impacts (e.g., individuals denied a loan) and indirect impacts (e.g., erosion of public trust in AI).

**2.3 Regulatory and Legal Consequences**
Research and describe the actual regulatory, legal, or reputational consequences the organization faced as a result of the failure. What investigations were launched? What fines or penalties were imposed? How did the organization's public reputation change?

*Marking Criteria: Depth and accuracy of root cause analysis (10 marks); comprehensiveness of stakeholder impact analysis (10 marks); accuracy of regulatory/legal consequences (10 marks).*

---

### Section 3: ISO/IEC 42001 Gap Analysis (30 Marks)

In this section, you must apply your knowledge of ISO/IEC 42001 to the case. Specifically, you must:

**3.1 Identify the AIMS Gaps**
Based on your research, identify at least **five specific clauses or requirements** of ISO/IEC 42001 that the organization failed to meet (or would have failed to meet, if the standard had existed at the time). For each gap:
- State the specific clause number and title.
- Explain the requirement in your own words.
- Provide evidence from your research that demonstrates how the organization failed to meet this requirement.
- Explain how meeting this requirement could have prevented or mitigated the failure.

Present your analysis in a structured table followed by a detailed narrative explanation for each gap.

**3.2 The Role of Annex A Controls**
Select **two specific controls from Annex A** of ISO/IEC 42001 that are most directly relevant to the governance failure you have analyzed. For each control:
- Identify the control and its objective.
- Explain how the absence of this control contributed to the failure.
- Describe what a robust implementation of this control would have looked like in practice for this specific organization.

*Marking Criteria: Accuracy and relevance of the five AIMS gaps (20 marks); depth of analysis for the two Annex A controls (10 marks).*

---

### Section 4: Proposed AIMS Implementation Strategy (20 Marks)

You have been engaged as an AI governance consultant by the organization in your case study. They have asked you to develop a high-level AIMS implementation strategy to prevent a recurrence of the governance failure and to achieve ISO/IEC 42001 certification within 18 months.

Your strategy must address the following:

**4.1 Immediate Priority Actions (0-3 Months)**
What are the three most critical actions the organization must take immediately to stop the harm and demonstrate accountability? These should be concrete, actionable steps, not vague platitudes.

**4.2 AIMS Foundation Building (3-9 Months)**
Describe the key activities required to establish the foundational elements of the AIMS. This must include, at a minimum:
- Establishing the organizational context and identifying interested parties (Clause 4).
- Developing and communicating an AI Policy (Clause 5).
- Conducting an initial AI Risk Assessment and AI System Impact Assessment (Clause 6).

**4.3 Operationalizing the AIMS (9-18 Months)**
Describe the key operational controls and monitoring mechanisms the organization must implement to operationalize the AIMS and prepare for certification. This must include, at a minimum:
- Specific operational controls for the AI system at the center of the failure (Clause 8).
- A plan for monitoring and measuring the AI system's performance, including specific fairness or safety metrics (Clause 9).
- A plan for the internal audit and management review cycle (Clauses 9.2 and 9.3).

*Marking Criteria: Practicality and specificity of immediate actions (5 marks); quality of AIMS foundation building plan (7 marks); quality of operationalization plan (8 marks).*

---

### Section 5: Critical Evaluation of ISO/IEC 42001 (10 Marks)

In this final section, you must step back and critically evaluate the standard itself. Your analysis must address the following question:

> **"Is ISO/IEC 42001 sufficient as a standalone governance framework to prevent the type of AI failure you have analyzed, or does effective AI governance require additional mechanisms beyond a management system standard?"**

Your response must:
- Present a balanced argument, acknowledging both the strengths and the limitations of ISO/IEC 42001.
- Reference at least two alternative or complementary AI governance frameworks (e.g., the EU AI Act, the NIST AI Risk Management Framework, the OECD AI Principles, or the UK AI Safety Institute's frameworks).
- Draw a clear, well-reasoned conclusion.

*Marking Criteria: Balance and depth of critical evaluation (5 marks); quality of comparison with alternative frameworks (5 marks).*
