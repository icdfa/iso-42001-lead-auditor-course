# Course Syllabus: ISO/IEC 42001 Lead Auditor

**Provider:** Center for Professional Development, International Cybersecurity and Digital Forensics Academy
**Format:** Self-Paced Learning
**Prerequisites:** Basic understanding of Artificial Intelligence concepts and general management system auditing (ISO 19011) is recommended but not strictly required.

## Course Description
This comprehensive self-paced course provides participants with the knowledge and skills required to perform first, second, and third-party audits of Artificial Intelligence Management Systems (AIMS) against ISO/IEC 42001:2023, in accordance with ISO 19011 and ISO/IEC 17021-1.

## Course Objectives
Upon completion of this course, participants will be able to:
1. Explain the purpose and business benefits of an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS).
2. Interpret the requirements of ISO/IEC 42001 in the context of an audit.
3. Plan, conduct, report, and follow up on an audit of an AIMS to establish conformity (or otherwise) with ISO/IEC 42001.
4. Apply the concepts of AI Risk Assessment and AI System Impact Assessment.
5. Formulate clear, objective, and actionable nonconformity reports.

## Course Structure

| Module | Topic | Key Focus Areas |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Module 1** | Introduction to AI & ISO/IEC 42001 | AI Evolution, Need for Governance, Standard Overview, Harmonized Structure. |
| **Module 2** | Fundamental Concepts of AIMS | PDCA Cycle, Key Terminology, Organizational Context, Leadership & Policy. |
| **Module 3** | Planning and Risk Management | Clause 6, AI Risk Assessment, Impact Assessment, Statement of Applicability (SoA). |
| **Module 4** | Support and Operation of AIMS | Resources, Competence, Documented Information, AI Lifecycle Operational Controls. |
| **Module 5** | Performance Evaluation & Improvement | Monitoring, Internal Audits, Management Review, Corrective Actions (Clauses 9 & 10). |
| **Module 6** | Audit Concepts & Principles | ISO 19011 Principles, Audit Types, Managing an Audit Program, Auditor Competence. |
| **Module 7** | Preparing for an Audit | Initiation, Stage 1 Document Review, Audit Planning, Checklists & Sampling. |
| **Module 8** | Conducting the Audit | Opening Meeting, Evidence Gathering (Interviews/Observation), Writing Nonconformities. |
| **Module 9** | Closing the Audit & Follow-up | Audit Conclusions, Closing Meeting, Formal Reporting, Reviewing Corrective Actions. |
| **Module 10** | Case Studies & Practice Exam | Real-world application scenarios and a comprehensive simulated certification exam. |

## Assessment
To gauge your readiness for formal certification, you must complete the comprehensive Practice Exam located in Module 10. A passing score is generally considered to be 70% or higher.

## Certification
*Note: Completion of this GitHub repository constitutes the educational component of Lead Auditor training. To become a formally certified ISO/IEC 42001 Lead Auditor, you must pass a proctored examination administered by a recognized, accredited certification body.*
